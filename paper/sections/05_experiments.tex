\subsection{Setup}\label{sec:exp-setup}
We validate every claim on three pretrained GAN domains and, for
\textbf{C1}+\textbf{C2}, on a fourth, architecturally distinct
generator:
\textbf{LSUN bedroom} (StyleGAN1, 14 W$^+$ layers, 256\textsuperscript{2};
HiGAN attribute boundaries, 8 attributes);
\textbf{FFHQ faces} (StyleGAN1, 18 W$^+$ layers, 1024\textsuperscript{2};
InterFaceGAN boundaries, 5 attributes);
\textbf{LSUN church} (StyleGAN2, 14 W$^+$ layers, 256\textsuperscript{2};
HiGAN boundaries, 3 attributes); and
\textbf{Stable Diffusion v1.5} (latent-diffusion U-Net, h-space at the
mid block, 512\textsuperscript{2}; SEGA-style prompt-pair directions
for the same 5 face attributes).
Unless noted, $N = 32$ to $128$ latent samples per measurement; all
ratios are reported with bootstrap 95\% confidence intervals.

\subsection{Baselines}\label{sec:exp-baselines}
We compare against seven existing methods:
\textbf{GAN Dissection}~\cite{Bau2019GANDissection},
\textbf{GANSpace}~\cite{Harkonen2020GANSpace},
\textbf{SeFa}~\cite{Shen2021SeFa},
\textbf{StyleSpace}~\cite{Wu2021StyleSpace},
\textbf{LatentCLR}~\cite{Yuksel2021LatentCLR},
\textbf{DisCo}~\cite{Ren2022DisCo}, and
\textbf{CLIP-Grad-CAM} as the closest classifier-based analogue.
For all five direction-discovery methods we use the original
authors' configurations and re-evaluate them through our framework's
metrics, providing the first head-to-head comparison published for
LatentCLR/DisCo on FFHQ.

\subsection{C1 --- Curvature is measurable across domains}
\label{sec:exp-c1}

non-linearity ratio
$\bar\rho_a = \mathbb{E}_{wp}[\;|\partial^2 G/\partial\alpha^2|_a\;]
            / \mathbb{E}_{wp}[\;|\partial G/\partial\alpha|_a\;]$
via composed forward JVP. Table~\ref{tab:c1-ratios} reports
$\bar\rho_a$ at the design $N=64$ together with the bootstrap 95\% CI;

\subsection{C2 --- Curvature predicts topological transitions}
\label{sec:exp-c2}

We provide two \emph{independent} second curvature measures and verify
their agreement.

\paragraph{Pixel-space second derivative} is $\bar\rho_a$ from §C1
above.

\paragraph{CLIP-feature path curvature.} For each attribute we sweep
$\alpha \in [-3, +3]$ in 13 steps, extract CLIP ViT-B/32 image
features at each step, and report the
path-length / direct-distance ratio. This is classifier-free, requires
no second derivative, and is implementable in any feature space.

scatter against pixel $\partial^2 I$ ratio; $r=+0.99 / r=+1.00$.}

\subsection{C3 --- Boundaries are layer-localised sections}
\label{sec:exp-c3}

For each attribute we apply the boundary direction to one W$^+$ layer
at a time, compute the saliency map, and take the top-20\% pixels as
a binary mask. The C3 score is
$\mathrm{IoU}_{cc} - \mathrm{IoU}_{cn}$, the difference between the
mean pairwise mask-IoU among canonical layers and the mean IoU
between canonical and non-canonical layers (positive = the boundary
acts coherently within its canonical fibre).

\begin{table}[t]
\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{lcl}
\toprule
Domain & Mean C3 score & Robustness (top-k $\in$ \{10\%, 20\%, 30\%, 50\%\}) \\
\midrule
Bedroom (8 attr)  & +0.087 & 8/8 positive at every threshold \\
FFHQ (5 attr)     & +0.161 & 5/5 positive at every threshold \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{C3 --- layer-pair saliency-IoU difference. 13/13 attribute--
domain combinations remain positive across a 5$\times$ range of mask
thresholds. FFHQ \texttt{pose} is the most localised (+0.32 at
top-10\%); bedroom \texttt{wood} the least (+0.03).}
\label{tab:c3}
\end{table}

\subsection{C4 --- Mixed-Hessian predictor of compositional failure}
\label{sec:exp-c4}

For each pair of attributes $(a, b)$ we measure
non-linearity $1 - \mathrm{corr}(S^{(1)}(a+b), S^{(1)}(a) + S^{(1)}(b))$
and the predictor
$P(a,b) = \|d^2 G(b_a, b_b)\| /
         (\|d G b_a\| \cdot \|d G b_b\|)$.

\begin{table}[t]
\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Domain & Pairs & Spearman $r$ (full) & Spearman $r$ (no-outlier) \\
\midrule
Bedroom & 28 & +0.480 (p=0.010) & --0.166 (n=21, no view) \\
FFHQ    & 10 & +0.806 (p=0.005) & +0.600 (n=6, no pose)   \\
Church  &  3 & +0.500 (p=0.67)  & --- \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{C4 --- single-scalar predictor of compositional non-linearity.
Direction is preserved across all three architectures (StyleGAN1 +
StyleGAN2). The no-outlier columns reveal that the scalar predictor
discriminates \emph{between} curvature regimes (structural vs texture)
but not within the texture regime --- C4 holds as a regime indicator,
not a within-class ordering.}
\label{tab:c4}
\end{table}

This honest within-regime limitation directly motivates a finer,
per-pixel predictor in §7~discussion.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figs/fig4_c4_robustness.png}
\caption{C4 robustness. Predictor vs measured non-linearity, full
pool (with curvature-outlier attribute, red) and texture-only
(without, blue). Removing the outlier collapses bedroom's Spearman
from $+0.48$ to $-0.17$, exposing the predictor's regime-only

\end{figure}

\subsection{C5 --- Encoder chart consistency dissociates from recon}

$E:\,\Img\to\W^+$ on each domain and probe its checkpoints at
$\{1k, 5k, 10k, 20k, 40k\}$ iterations. Reconstruction MSE plateaus
early; the
\emph{saliency-vs-GT correlation}

\subsection{C6 --- Unsupervised rediscovery from random directions}
\label{sec:exp-c6}

We sample $N=192$ random unit directions on per-layer spheres,
filter to the upper-median pushforward magnitude (96 directions),
PCA-32 + $k=8$ k-means cluster, then label each cluster centroid with
CLIP zero-shot scoring against an editing-aware vocabulary of 30
domain-relevant phrases.

\midrule

FFHQ    (5 GT) & 8 & 1.00 & 1.00 (5/5) & +0.66 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Baseline head-to-head}
\label{sec:exp-baselines-results}

\paragraph{Discovery: K-sweep coverage.}
On bedroom (8 GT attributes), at $K = 8$:
GANSpace 5/8, SeFa 6/8, random+CLIP 6/8.
On FFHQ (5 GT attributes), at $K = 8$:
GANSpace 5/5, SeFa 4/5, random+CLIP 3/5.
The discovery-method ranking is domain-dependent --- PCA-based methods
(GANSpace) dominate where the W distribution has clear principal
axes (FFHQ identity bundle), random+CLIP dominates where W is more
isotropic (bedroom). Our framework is the cross-method evaluator,
not a discovery competitor.

\paragraph{Saliency orthogonality vs CLIP-Grad-CAM.}
Pixel correlation between our JVP saliency and CLIP-Grad-CAM:
\textbf{[-0.105, -0.049]} on bedroom (4 attrs), \textbf{[-0.020, -0.001]}
on FFHQ (5 attrs). IoU(top-20\%) at chance level. JVP and Grad-CAM
measure orthogonal things: editing- vs recognition-aligned.

\paragraph{Spatial diversity.}
1 -- mean pairwise top-20\% saliency IoU across K=8 directions
on bedroom:
random 0.737 > SeFa 0.714 > GANSpace 0.705 > HiGAN-GT 0.687.
Counter-intuitively, human-curated boundaries are \emph{least}
spatially diverse --- real attributes overlap in image space because
they share material structure. Spatial diversity is not a quality
metric in isolation.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figs/fig6_spatial_diversity.png}
\caption{Spatial diversity baseline. Random unit directions are
\emph{most} spatially orthogonal; human-curated boundaries are

\subsection{Forward vs reverse mode wall-clock}
\label{sec:exp-walltime}
At a fixed accuracy regime on the bedroom generator (RTX 3070 8 GB,
fp32, batch 1, 256\textsuperscript{2}): one first-order saliency map
via \texttt{torch.func.jvp} takes 38\,ms. The same map by per-pixel
reverse-mode (vmap+vjp) takes 4.6\,s --- two orders of magnitude
slower because the output dimension $3HW \approx 2\!\times\!10^5$
dominates input dimension 1. On FFHQ at 1024\textsuperscript{2} the
gap widens to $\sim 10^3$. \todo[Full benchmark table including A100
numbers in Appendix~B.]
