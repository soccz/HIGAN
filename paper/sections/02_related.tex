\paragraph{GAN interpretation and attribute editing.}
\textbf{Unit-level / segmentation-based dissection}
\cite{Bau2019GANDissection} probes individual generator units via
attached segmentation classifiers. \textbf{Boundary-based editing}
\cite{Shen2020InterFaceGAN, Yang2020HiGAN} learns linear hyperplanes
in $W^+$ for semantic attributes; we use the resulting boundaries as
inputs to our analysis. \textbf{Unsupervised discovery} approaches —
PCA on activations~\cite{Harkonen2020GANSpace}, closed-form factorisation
of the affine layer~\cite{Shen2021SeFa}, and channel-wise style
manipulation~\cite{Wu2021StyleSpace} — find directions without
labels; we compare against these on rediscovery of hand-curated
boundaries.

\paragraph{Classical saliency.}
CAM~\cite{Zhou2016CAM} and Grad-CAM~\cite{Selvaraju2017GradCAM}
attribute classifier decisions to spatial regions via either pooled
weights or class-score gradients. Our pushforward saliency parallels
Grad-CAM in spirit but operates on the generator alone, with no
classifier. We discuss this distinction in \S\ref{sec:method} and
include real Grad-CAM (through a CLIP-distilled attribute classifier)
as a baseline in \S\ref{sec:experiments}.

\paragraph{Differential geometry of neural networks.}
Natural gradient methods~\cite{Amari1998NaturalGradient} and
geometric deep learning~\cite{Bronstein2021GeometricDL} formalise the
ambient geometry of \emph{parameter} space and \emph{input} domains.
Our setting is different: we study the geometry of the manifold of
\emph{outputs} learned by a fixed generator. Closest in spirit is
\cite{Zhu2016ManipulationOnManifold}, which introduced the "natural
image manifold" formulation; we provide a tractable curvature
estimator on this manifold.

\paragraph{Forward-mode autodifferentiation.}
The Pearlmutter trick~\cite{Pearlmutter1994HVP} for Hessian-vector
products underlies modern second-order optimisation
\cite{Martens2015KFAC} and is implemented as \texttt{torch.func.jvp}
and \texttt{jax.jvp}~\cite{PyTorch2023functorch}. To our knowledge no
prior work applies \emph{composed} forward-mode \jvp to GAN latent
spaces for per-pixel curvature and compositional editing
prediction.

\paragraph{Other related directions.}
Compositional editing approaches such as
GANalyze~\cite{Goetschalckx2019GANalyze} and steerable
GANs~\cite{Jahanian2020Steerability} explicitly treat latent
manipulations as vector fields. EditGAN~\cite{Ling2021EditGAN}
performs non-linear edits using segmentation supervision.
StyleCLIP~\cite{Patashnik2021StyleCLIP} uses CLIP for text-driven
editing; we use CLIP only to label automatically discovered
clusters. A full survey of $\sim$60 references is provided in the
supplementary material.
