\begin{abstract}
We propose viewing trained GAN latent spaces through a differential-geometric
lens: the generator $G : \W \to \Img$ defines an image manifold $\Mfd = G(\W)$,
and many ``interpretation'' tasks reduce to classical geometric operations on
this manifold — pushforward of tangent vectors (per-pixel saliency), second-order
pushforward (curvature), Lie brackets of attribute vector fields (compositional
editing failure), and stratification by tangent-space clustering (unsupervised
attribute discovery). We argue that \emph{forward-mode automatic differentiation}
is the natural and efficient computational tool: it gives all per-pixel
sensitivities in one pass and composes cleanly to second order. We formalise
six structural claims about trained GAN manifolds, validate each
quantitatively on three generative domains (LSUN bedroom, FFHQ faces, LSUN
church), and present a single use case — \emph{predicting compositional
editing interference from manifold curvature} — that benefits from the
unified framework. Code and reproducible benchmarks at
\url{https://github.com/soccz/HIGAN}.
\end{abstract}
